What is CKD?

Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) means your kidneys are damaged and canâ€™t filter blood the way they should. This damage can cause waste to build up in your body.

Stages of CKD

CKD progresses through stages 1 to 5, based on how well your kidneys are functioning (GFR levels).

  • Stage 1: Normal function but evidence of kidney damage
  • Stage 2: Mild reduction in kidney function
  • Stage 3: Moderate reduction
  • Stage 4: Severe reduction
  • Stage 5: Kidney failure (dialysis or transplant needed)

Risk Factors

Common risk factors include diabetes, high blood pressure, family history, obesity, and smoking.
